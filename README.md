## Project Overview
This repository contains my Digital Literacy Project completed as part of the course requirements. The aim of this project is to understand digital literacy in a practical way by exploring online tools, professional communication, safe internet practices, digital portfolio building, and cybercrime awareness. Through these tasks, I learned how digital platforms can be used responsibly, effectively, and professionally in academic life.

## Module Summaries

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
In this task, I created a one-page visual resource explaining what digital literacy means and why it matters. The infographic covered key areas such as useful digital tools for students, safe internet practices, professional online presence, and email etiquette.

### Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio
In this task, I created a basic digital portfolio using platforms such as GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. This helped me understand how students can build a professional online identity and prepare for future academic and career opportunities.

### Task 3 – Coding Practice and Google Workspace Collaboration
In this task, I used HackerRank to complete a beginner-level coding challenge and used Google Forms to create a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz. I also linked the form to Google Sheets to understand response collection and collaboration tools.

### Task 4 – Professional Emails and Social Media Etiquette
In this task, I wrote two professional emails and prepared a social media do's and don'ts checklist. This helped me understand the importance of clear, respectful, and professional digital communication.

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study and Prevention Guide
In this task, I created a case study on UPI/Online Payment Fraud and prepared a prevention checklist. This helped me understand common online threats and the importance of safe digital habits.
