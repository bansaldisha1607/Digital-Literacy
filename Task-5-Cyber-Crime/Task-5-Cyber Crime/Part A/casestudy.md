# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI and online payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. In this type of fraud, criminals trick people into sharing payment details or approving fake transactions. A realistic example is when a student receives a call or message claiming that they have won a refund, scholarship amount, or online shopping reward. The fraudster sounds convincing and creates urgency so that the victim acts quickly.

The scam usually happens step by step. First, the victim gets a phone call, WhatsApp message, or SMS with a fake offer or warning. Second, the scammer asks the victim to click a link, scan a QR code, share an OTP, or approve a collect request in a UPI app. Third, the victim follows the instructions, believing that money will be received. In reality, the action authorizes money to leave the account. Within minutes, the amount is transferred to the fraudster.

Students, elderly people, first-time digital payment users, and people who panic easily are common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, data theft, and reduced trust in online payment systems. This case shows that people must never share OTPs, approve unknown UPI requests, or scan QR codes without verification.
